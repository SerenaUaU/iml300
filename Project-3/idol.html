Idols may appear to hold power—enjoying fame, visibility, and financial success—but their position is far more fragile than it seems. Their careers are deeply dependent on fans’ attention, approval, and continuous support, which means they are constantly adjusting themselves to meet expectations. At the same time, they use carefully crafted intimacy to guide fans’ emotions and loyalty.
As a result, the relationship is not simply top-down: idols are both beneficiaries of fandom and shaped by it, caught in a complex and ongoing negotiation of power.
